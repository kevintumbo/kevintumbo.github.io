About Global Leadership for Peace & Reconciliation

Global Leadership for Peace & Reconciliation, Inc. (Lead for Peace)

is a non-profit organization dedicated to shaping leaders into strong advocates for peace and security.

We aim to mitigate the cycle of violence by transforming the structures, policies, and practices that perpetuate it.

Our mission is to equip leaders to become catalysts of peace and security by transforming structures, policies, and practices that drive violence, ensuring that all citizens can safely and peacefully achieve their full potential.

Our Team

Dr. Celestin Musekura

President/CEO

Dr. Célestin Musekura is an Adjunct Professor of Cross-Cultural Management Analysis and Africa Regional Management Area Studies at the University of Texas at Dallas' Jindal School of Management. He is also the Founder, President, and CEO of African Leadership and Reconciliation Ministries (ALARM), an organization he established in response to the 1994 genocide in Rwanda. ALARM equips church leaders, pastors, and Christian professionals to confront issues such as nominal Christianity, ethnic violence, and tribalism in East and Central Africa. The ministry focuses on training individuals in biblical servant leadership, peacebuilding, conflict resolution, mediation, forgiveness, and reconciliation.


Dr. Musekura holds a Master of Science in Justice Administration and Leadership from the University of Texas at Dallas, a Master of Divinity from Africa International University in Nairobi, Kenya, and a PhD in Theological Studies from Dallas Theological Seminary, specializing in communal forgiveness. He is dedicated to developing servant leaders for reconciliation in regions that have experienced genocide and mass human atrocities, including Rwanda, the Democratic Republic of Congo, Burundi, South Sudan, Uganda, and Sudan. Through ALARM, Dr. Musekura trains religious leaders and Christian professionals—such as judges, lawyers, and lawmakers—in peacebuilding, conflict resolution, mediation, restorative justice, trauma healing, recovery, and interfaith dialogue, with a focus on fostering peaceful coexistence in East and Central Africa.


In 2020, Dr. Musekura founded the Global Leadership for Peace and Reconciliation (Lead for Peace), with the mission of building a global community of leaders empowered to promote peace and security for their citizens. The organization's vision is a world free from violent conflict, achieved through peacebuilding, restorative justice, conflict mediation and transformation, communal forgiveness and reconciliation.


Dr. Musekura is passionate about sharing his expertise and experiences in peacebuilding, conflict transformation, forgiveness, and reconciliation in East and Central Africa with global leaders facing violence and unrest in their nations. He offers unique insights into the challenges and opportunities for reconciliation in areas affected by violence and injustice, providing lessons applicable to both social relationships and larger societal healing efforts.

NSENGIYUMVA Jean

Vice President, Programs and Development

NSENGIYUMVA Jean is a Rwandan finance and development professional with more than two decades of experience in financial management, accounting, and both financial and organizational auditing. He currently works as an Independent consultant in management and External Auditor.


His academic background in economics began in 1982 at the Adventist College of Rwankeri. He holds a Bachelor’s degree in Management and Economic Sciences from the University of CEPROMAD-GOMA in the Democratic Republic of Congo (2001). His academic interests focus on the intersection of economic theory and public policy, particularly in the context of poverty reduction in Rwanda.


In 2022, he completed a Master’s degree in Project Management at the Institut Supérieur de Sciences Sociales, de la Santé et de la Population (ISSP) in Bobo Dioulasso, Burkina Faso, where he deepened his engagement with sustainable development issues from a global perspective.


Since the 2024–2025 academic year, he has been pursuing a PhD in Development Project Management at Cheikh Anta Diop University (UCAD) in Dakar, Senegal. His research and professional objectives are centered on promoting equity and sustainable development. He upholds the belief that economists and managers must not only analyze the world but also actively contribute to its transformation.

Erica Asante

Erica Asante is a dynamic global health leader, consultant, and author with over 15 years of experience designing, managing, and evaluating public health programs across sub-Saharan Africa, Southeast Asia, and the United States. Her cross-sector career spans non-profits, academia, and government, with a focus on strengthening health systems, advancing health equity, and fostering strategic partnerships.


She has led global partnerships across five continents and managed more than $30 million in funding from institutions including the Gates Foundation, NIH, PEPFAR, and GlaxoSmithKline. Erica has directed cervical cancer prevention programs across Africa, supported national immunization campaigns, launched cross-border medical education exchanges, and advised Ministries of Health on program implementation and policy development.


As a consultant, Erica supports academic institutions, philanthropic organizations, and global health NGOs in areas such as strategic planning, partnership development, program design, and monitoring and evaluation. Her expertise is particularly sought after for projects involving women’s health, global research capacity building, and cross-cultural collaboration.


Outside of her public health work, Erica is the author of Threads of Me: Kente for Show and Tell, a children's book that celebrates cultural heritage and the importance of sharing our traditions with others. As a speaker, she engages audiences on topics including global health equity, storytelling for social change, and building impactful careers in public health.


Erica holds a Master of Public Health in Global Health Program Design, Monitoring, and Evaluation from The George Washington University, a Bachelor’s in Government and Politics from the University of Maryland, and a Certificate in Non-Profit Leadership from Southern Methodist University.

Dr. Moses John Monday

Africa Director of Programmes and Development

Dr. Moses John Monday is a seasoned development practitioner with over 20 years of experience leading peacebuilding, humanitarian, and development initiatives across South Sudan, Sudan, and other African countries. He co-founded and formerly led the Organization for Nonviolence and Development (ONAD) in Juba and currently serves as Co-chair of the Pan African Nonviolence and Peacebuilding Network (PANPEN).


Dr. John has held senior roles with national and international NGOs and served on the Council of the International Fellowship of Reconciliation (2014–2020). He is a member of the Advisory Board for the Global Peacebuilders’ Summit (Germany) and actively connects peace practitioners with researchers and training opportunities across the continent.


He lectures and facilitates workshops globally on peacebuilding, governance, nonviolence, and leadership. Dr. John holds a PhD in Public Administration from Durban University of Technology, and multiple postgraduate degrees in peace and development.


His latest publication explores climate change, food security, and peace in East Africa, featured in a 2024 Springer volume on sustainable development in the Anthropocene.
